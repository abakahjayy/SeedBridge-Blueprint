Part I: The Challenge Brief
The original parameters provided by the Ghana Data Science Summit (GDSS) and GIZ.
Challenge Overview
Challenge Title: Farmer-to-Buyer Digital Marketplace Platform
Prizes:
1st Prize: GHS 10,000 | 2nd Prize: GHS 7,000 | 3rd Prize: GHS 5,000
The Problem
Agriculture remains one of the most important sectors of Ghana’s economy, contributing
approximately 20% of the country’s GDP and employing more than 30% of the national workforce.
The sector is dominated by smallholder and small-to-medium scale farmers, with over 80% of
farms in Ghana operating on less than five acres of land.
Despite its importance, small-to-medium scale farmers growing perishable vegetables, particularly
tomatoes, peppers, garden eggs, okra, and leafy greens face challenges in accessing reliable
markets and fair pricing. The time-sensitive nature of perishable produce makes inefficiencies in
market access especially costly.
In addition, even when farmers successfully connect with buyers, transporting produce efficiently
and affordably remains a major challenge. Poor logistics coordination, lack of reliable transport
options, and fragmented delivery systems often lead to delays, spoilage, increased costs, and lost
income.
Data Impact: Ghana is estimated to lose between 20% and 50% of fruits and vegetables after
harvest due to poor storage systems, transportation delays, and weak distribution networks. These
losses are estimated to cost the country between $1.9 billion and $3 billion annually.
At the same time, buyers such as retailers, restaurants, processors, exporters, and households
struggle to identify trusted suppliers, compare prices, locate nearby produce, and coordinate timely
delivery of goods.
2
Proposed Solution & Core Features
Develop a digital platform that connects smallholder vegetable farmers directly to buyers through a
mobile or web application, while also enabling efficient coordination of transport and delivery.
Farmer Features: Registration, profile creation, produce listing (images, quantity, location,
pricing), inventory updates, communication with buyers, ability to request transport.
Buyer Features: Registration, search/filtering, direct ordering/negotiation, order history, delivery
coordination/tracking.
Logistics Features: Transport provider registration, booking system, matching farmers/buyers
with nearby transport, delivery scheduling/route coordination, tracking, notifications.
Generic Features: Product listing, geolocation, in-app messaging, rating system, mobile
money/payment integration, dashboard monitoring.
Deliverables & Timelines
A working prototype or MVP (Frontend mobile/web + Backend/database system).
A live demo of the platform workflow (registration, search, end-to-end marketplace operations).
Bonus: Transport request and delivery/logistics coordination flow.
Activity Dates
Registration deadline 26 June 2026
Challenge period 29 June – 10 July 2026
First round of judging 13 July – 17 July 2026
Final presentation & Awards 24 July 2026

Part II: The SeedBridge Masterplan
The strategic, gap-free blueprint to win the hackathon by solving the core issues of information
asymmetry, empty-return transport costs, and perishability.
1. Target Market Strategy
Region: Eastern Region (Suhum / Yilo-Krobo)
Target Crops: Tomatoes, Garden Eggs, and Okra
Rationale: The Eastern Region is a major vegetable production hub located just 1.5 to 2 hours
from Accra. However, due to poor feeder roads and uncoordinated logistics, post-harvest losses
remain massive. Tomatoes are highly perishable, making them the ultimate stress-test for a
logistics platform. Solving for tomatoes proves the platform's robustness.
2. Core Innovations (The Winning Edge)
1. Pre-Harvest Matching Engine (Eliminating Wait-Time Rot)
The Gap: Waiting to find a buyer after harvesting guarantees post-harvest loss.
The Solution: SeedBridge operates a predictive marketplace. Farmers upload expected
yields and harvest dates up to two weeks in advance. Buyers place deposits to lock in the
produce.
Impact: Farmers harvest only what is sold, reducing wait-time PHL to near 0%.
4
2. "Milk-Run" & Backhaul Logistics
The Gap: Trucks waste fuel driving empty to rural areas and making multiple small stops.
The Solution: The app batches orders via Virtual Aggregation. Multiple farmers deliver to a
central community pickup point at a scheduled time, allowing drivers to make a single stop.
Furthermore, the Backhaul Coordination feature lets agro-chemical suppliers book empty
trucks returning to the rural areas.
Impact: Transport costs drop drastically for farmers as drivers monetize both legs of the
journey.
3. Co-Opting "Market Queens"
The Gap: Tech often fails by trying to bypass traditional market authorities.
The Solution: SeedBridge embraces them via an Agent/Aggregator Dashboard. Traditional
market queens can log in to aggregate demand for multiple street vendors, utilizing their
existing capital and trust.
Impact: Instant, massive user adoption without fighting existing cultural market structures.
4. "Fresh Rescue" Algorithmic Pricing
The Gap: Unsold tomatoes rot completely, yielding zero revenue.
The Solution: When produce nears its maximum shelf-life (e.g., 48 hours post-harvest), the
platform automatically applies an algorithmic discount, shifting the listing to a "Fresh Rescue"
tier.
Impact: Commercial agro-processors (tomato paste factories) get instant alerts for
processing-grade produce at cheap rates, allowing the farmer to recover some capital.
5. Mobile Money (MoMo) Escrow
The Gap: Lack of trust between remote farmers and urban buyers.

Frontend Stack (UI/UX Focus)
Framework: Next.js (App Router) for superior routing and rendering.
Styling: Tailwind CSS paired with shadcn/ui for rapid, accessible, and professional components
without writing CSS from scratch.
Data Fetching & State: TanStack Query (React Query) to seamlessly consume APIs, manage
loading states, and handle caching.
Form Validation: React Hook Form + Zod to ensure clean database entries.
Backend Stack (Data & API Focus)
Framework: Node.js / Express acting as the standalone API server.
Database: MongoDB for flexible schema design (ideal for rapid hackathon changes).
Integration: Exposing REST endpoints (`/api/produce`, `/api/logistics`) for the Next.js frontend
to consume via Server Components.
